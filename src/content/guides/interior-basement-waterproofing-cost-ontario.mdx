Interior basement waterproofing is one of the most variable renovation categories for pricing in Ontario. The range is wide because the scope genuinely varies by home. Here is how to understand what you are actually paying for.

## Typical Cost Ranges

**Per linear foot:** $150–$350 per linear foot of waterproofed perimeter. This is the most accurate way to price waterproofing because the dominant cost driver is perimeter length.

**Full project totals:**

| Home Size | Approx. Perimeter | Estimated Cost |
|---|---|---|
| Small semi or row home (900 sq ft) | 110–130 linear ft | $8,000–$18,000 |
| Mid-size detached (1,200 sq ft) | 140–160 linear ft | $12,000–$22,000 |
| Large detached (1,600+ sq ft) | 180–220 linear ft | $18,000–$30,000 |

These ranges assume a complete system: weeping tile, crushed stone drainage bed, dimpled membrane, sump pit, and primary plus battery backup pump. Projects that exclude the membrane or backup pump will quote lower but deliver less.

## What Drives the Variance

**Perimeter length:** The dominant variable. A 3-bedroom semi in Leslieville has roughly 115 linear feet of waterproofable perimeter. A detached home in North York may have 200 linear feet. The materials and labour scale with length.

**Sump pit:** If your home has no existing sump pit, installation adds $800–$1,500 to the project cost. If there is an existing pit, the contractor assesses whether it can be reused or needs replacement.

**Pump specification:** A quality primary submersible pump costs $300–$600 installed. A battery backup unit adds $600–$1,200 depending on capacity. Some quotes use entry-level pumps — ask specifically which pump models are specified.

**Membrane inclusion:** Some contractors quote weeping tile and stone only, without the dimpled wall membrane. The membrane adds $12–$25 per linear foot but is essential for capturing water that enters above footing level through the wall face. Always confirm whether membrane is included.

**Concrete restoration:** The slab restoration after weeping tile installation is sometimes quoted separately. Confirm whether the per-linear-foot rate includes saw-cut, break-out, installation, and slab restoration in one number.

**Access difficulty:** Basements with low ceilings, finished walls that need to be removed, or access only through a narrow door or window add mobilization and demolition costs. These should be itemized separately.

<Callout type="tip" title="How to Compare Quotes Accurately">
  Ask every contractor to break down the quote by: (1) weeping tile installation per LF, (2) membrane per LF, (3) sump pit and pump installation, (4) slab restoration, and (5) any other items. A lower total may mean a component has been omitted. You cannot fairly compare a $9,000 quote that excludes membrane and backup pump to a $15,000 quote that includes both.
</Callout>

## What Is Not Included in a Standard Waterproofing Quote

Understand what you are buying before signing:

*   **Exterior excavation or exterior membrane** — interior waterproofing does not address the exterior wall face
*   **Structural crack repair** — active cracks that need injection or patching are typically a separate line item
*   **Mold remediation** — if there is existing mold behind finished walls, that is a separate scope
*   **Backwater valve installation** — sewer backup protection requires a separate backwater valve, which is eligible for the Toronto subsidy but is priced separately
*   **Finishing restoration** — if finished drywall walls need to be removed for access and re-installed after, that is typically not included
