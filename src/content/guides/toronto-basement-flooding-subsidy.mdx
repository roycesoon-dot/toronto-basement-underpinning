The City of Toronto offers financial subsidies for homeowners who install flood protection equipment. If you are installing a sump pump or backwater valve as part of a basement waterproofing or underpinning project, claiming the subsidy is a straightforward process that can offset $1,750 to $6,300 of your project cost.

## What the Subsidy Covers

The City of Toronto Basement Flooding Protection Subsidy Program covers three eligible items:

| Item | Maximum Subsidy |
|---|---|
| Sump pump (with or without pit) | $1,750 |
| Backwater valve (installed on main drain) | $1,750 |
| Foundation drain disconnection from sanitary sewer | Up to $2,800 |
| **Maximum per property** | **$6,300** |

The subsidy is a rebate — you pay the contractor's invoice in full and then apply to the City for reimbursement.

## Eligibility Requirements

**Property eligibility:**
*   Residential property within the City of Toronto (not Mississauga, Brampton, or other municipalities — each has its own program)
*   Property must have experienced basement flooding, or be located in a designated flood-risk area, OR be voluntarily installing flood protection equipment (since 2015, the program was expanded to all residential properties)
*   The property must not have a prior subsidy claim for the same equipment

**Equipment eligibility:**
*   **Sump pump:** Must be a submersible or pedestal pump installed in a properly constructed sump pit. Battery backup pumps are eligible if installed as part of a new sump system.
*   **Backwater valve:** Must be installed on the main sanitary drain line by a licensed plumber. Flap-type or gate-type valves meeting Ontario Building Code requirements qualify.
*   **Foundation drain disconnection:** Must disconnect the foundation weeping tile drain from the sanitary sewer and connect it to the sump pit instead. Requires a building permit and licensed plumber.

**Installation requirements:**
*   Work must be completed by licensed contractors (plumber for drain work, electrician for electrical connections)
*   Building permit required for foundation drain disconnection
*   Work must be completed before applying (receipts and photos required)

## How to Apply

**Step 1:** Complete the installation and obtain all receipts and contractor invoices.

**Step 2:** Take photographs of the installed equipment showing the installation location.

**Step 3:** Obtain a copy of any building permits and inspection certificates (for drain disconnection).

**Step 4:** Submit the application online at toronto.ca or by mail to:
City of Toronto, Basement Flooding Protection Subsidy
Toronto Water, 5100 Yonge Street, Toronto, ON M2N 5V7

**Step 5:** The City reviews the application and issues a cheque for the approved subsidy amount. Processing typically takes 6 to 12 weeks.

<Callout type="info" title="Apply Promptly — Budget Is Limited">
  The City of Toronto's subsidy program is funded annually and applications are processed on a first-come, first-served basis. Once the annual budget is exhausted, applications are deferred to the following year. Apply as soon as the installation is complete.
</Callout>

## Maximizing Your Subsidy

If you are already doing a basement underpinning or waterproofing project, add the backwater valve installation to the scope. The incremental cost of installing a backwater valve during a project where the main drain is already accessible is $800 to $1,500. The subsidy covers up to $1,750 — making it net positive in most cases.

The same logic applies to the foundation drain disconnection: if your original weeping tile still runs to the sanitary sewer (common in pre-1980 Toronto homes), disconnecting it during a waterproofing project and connecting it to the new sump pit costs $1,500 to $2,500 incremental and is eligible for up to $2,800 in subsidy.
