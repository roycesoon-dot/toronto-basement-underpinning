Foundation repair spans the widest cost range of any category in residential construction — from a few hundred dollars for a crack injection to six figures for full structural remediation. Here is a clear breakdown by repair type.

## Cost by Repair Type

### Polyurethane Crack Injection

**Cost:** $500–$2,500 per crack, depending on crack length and depth.

**What it includes:** Surface preparation, drilling of injection ports at 200–300 mm intervals along the crack, pressure injection of flexible polyurethane resin, port removal and surface finish.

**What affects cost:** Crack length (longer cracks require more ports and material), accessibility (cracks behind finished walls require drywall removal), and whether epoxy (more expensive, more rigid) or polyurethane is specified.

### Wall Anchor or Strap System (for lateral movement)

**Cost:** $4,000–$12,000 for a typical installation.

**What it includes:** Installation of steel wall anchors or carbon fibre straps into the basement wall and tied to stable soil or the floor slab, to arrest inward wall movement.

**What affects cost:** Number of anchors required (typically 4 to 8 for a standard wall section), soil conditions for anchor depth, and whether excavation is needed.

### Footing Repair (Localized)

**Cost:** $8,000–$25,000 for a single failing footing section.

**What it includes:** Localized excavation to expose the failing footing section, removal of deteriorated concrete, new footing pour at the original or extended depth, structural engineer inspection and sign-off.

**What affects cost:** Depth of the failing section, access to the exterior, soil conditions, whether the repair is interior or requires exterior excavation.

### Full Underpinning

**Cost:** $25,000–$80,000+ for full perimeter.

**What it includes:** Permits, structural engineering drawings, staged pin-section excavation and pouring around the full foundation perimeter, new slab restoration. See [basement underpinning cost guide](/guide/basement-underpinning-cost-ontario/) for the full breakdown.

**What affects cost:** Perimeter length, depth of extension required, soil conditions, urban access constraints.

## What to Watch For in Foundation Repair Quotes

**Quotes that skip the structural engineer:** Any work involving footing repair, wall movement, or differential settlement should include engineer involvement. A contractor who never mentions the engineer on a structurally significant repair is a red flag.

**Low crack injection quotes without site visits:** Legitimate crack injection requires measuring the crack, assessing depth and activity, and specifying the right injection material. A quote from a photo without a site visit is unreliable.

**Pressure to commit before an engineer reviews:** A reputable contractor on a significant foundation repair presents the scope and then encourages you to get the structural engineer's sign-off before signing. Anyone who discourages independent engineering review is protecting their own margin, not your foundation.
